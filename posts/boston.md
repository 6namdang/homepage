Today, I traveled all the way to Boston, Massachussets from Ames, Iowa to join a hackathon at MIT (after being rejected at MIT). Let me tell you what happened.

At the beginnning, when I began to board, the plane (AA) took more than 30 minutes to fly. The worst thing is that the plane keeps shaking like crazy, and I almost vomit. The shake was so bad that my brain hurts, which by the way, I never experienced this before. I am not a weak person, but I feel such headache.

Right after this flight (which was from Des Moines, Iowa to NYC LaGuardia (LGA)), I had to board to another plane from LaGuardia to Boston, MA. This plane changed gate 3 times, from 11, to 27, and then to 19. If I didn't check my phone, I would not be able to board this.

After that, the plane also shakes like crazy, which make me wanted to vomit as well. Upon landing, I rush to book an AirBnB, because I was so tired. Guess what? I forgot my charger on the airplane, and my phone is 5%. Wow. I told myself that I am the dumbest person on the entire planet. My plan was to charge my phone to 50%, then figure out a way to take the train and at the same time, view the city. I wanted to see the old-school buildings architecture of Boston, but now I had 2 choices. Either I book an Uber (which costs me 30$), or I can blindly take the train and hope that I remembered the destination correctly. So I chose the other one.

At the same time, I knew that my phone was going to die, so I copy all the passcode of the AirBnB into my flight ticket with a pen, so that way if my phone died while riding Uber, I can still be able to get to my room.

Guess what? I forgot to write down the passcode to the entrance, so when I arrived at my AirBnB, I panicked because the password I entered is incorrect (this is the password for my room).
So there I go. No internet. No wifi. No Phone. No way to contact the owner. I desperately bang my head into the wall, screaming. 

After a while, I calm down and try to find an answer. I wandered around the city, and found Target. Yes, I screamed inside, knowing that they have a charger. 

After buying the charger, I went to Chitpotle, where I was so lucky that the employee allowed me to charge my phone while I was eating there. So while the phone was charging, I was eating alone under the busy street of Boston. As much as I thought that I was a loser, I realized: Hey, no one really cares. When I imagine someone look at me, the thought that came out of my head was: "It just a guy eating". Wow. I realized that no one cares.

So I finish my bowl of chicken (which was amazing btw), and head back to the store to grab my phone. 

After checking with the owner, I went back to my place, and enter the password. Now, it's correct.

So here I am, talking about all of this to you guys. I hope you enjoy my ups and downs.
